# Inspection requirement templates

Copy-paste starting points for common gear, entered per asset on its
equipment page (**⚙ Manage requirements**) or via the API.

> ## ⚠ These are examples, not advice
>
> **Intervals and applicability vary by regulation, jurisdiction,
> manufacturer, and your company's own safety program.** The intervals
> below are placeholders to show the shape of a requirement — several are
> deliberately conservative, others may be too lax for your equipment or
> your state plan. Before using any of them, set the interval and
> reference from **your** written safety program, the manufacturer's
> manual for **your** model, and the regulations that apply to **your**
> work. TrenchNote records inspections; it is not a safety program and
> does not make anything compliant (ADR 0014).

| Gear | Requirement name | Placeholder interval | Reference to verify |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fire extinguisher | Monthly visual | 30 days | OSHA 1910.157(e)(2); NFPA 10 |
| Fire extinguisher | Annual maintenance check | 365 days | OSHA 1910.157(e)(3); NFPA 10 |
| Full-body harness | Pre-use check by the user | 1 day¹ | OSHA 1926.502; manufacturer manual |
| Full-body harness | Periodic competent-person inspection | 180 days | Manufacturer manual; ANSI Z359 program |
| Sling (synthetic/wire/chain) | Periodic thorough inspection | 365 days | OSHA 1910.184(d)–(e); ASME B30.9 |
| Gas monitor (4-gas) | Bump test / calibration | 30 days² | Manufacturer manual; company confined-space program |
| Extension ladder | Periodic inspection | 90 days | OSHA 1926.1053(b)(15); ANSI A14 |
| Crane / hoist | Annual thorough inspection | 365 days | OSHA 1926.1412(f); manufacturer |

¹ Pre-use checks are usually *per shift or before each use* — a 1-day
interval makes the badge nag daily, which may be more noise than your
program wants in a visibility tool. Many programs track pre-use checks on
paper/toolbox talks and put only the *periodic competent-person*
inspection in TrenchNote. Your call, from your program.

² Many manufacturers call for bump tests before each day's use and
calibration on a longer cycle — check the manual for your monitor.

## Entering one

On the asset's page: **⚙ Manage requirements** → name, interval in days,
reference → **Add requirement**. The asset immediately shows a RED
"never inspected" badge until its first passing inspection is logged —
log the inspection that justified adding the requirement and it turns
green.
